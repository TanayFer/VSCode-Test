12 Heritage Sites

Salher Fort, perched in the Selbari range of Nashik, is the highest fort in the Western Ghats and the site of a legendary 1671 battle where the Marathas triumphed over the Mughals. Its vast expanse and panoramic views made it a strategic watchtower.
Shivneri Fort, located near Junnar in Pune, is revered as the birthplace of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Surrounded by steep cliffs and fortified gates, it was designed to be impenetrable and served as a cradle of Maratha leadership.
Lohgad Fort, nestled near Lonavala, is famed for its "Vinchu Kata" (scorpion tail)-shaped ramparts. Used to store treasure looted from Surat, it was also key to controlling nearby trade routes. The fort’s lush surroundings make it especially popular during the monsoon season.
Khanderi Fort, in Raigad district off the coast of Mumbai, served as Shivaji’s maritime outpost. With its lighthouse and cannons, it was pivotal for monitoring naval activity and safeguarding the coastline from European intrusion.
Raigad Fort is perhaps the most iconic—Shivaji’s capital and the site of his coronation. Perched atop a cliff, it houses the king’s throne, queens’ quarters, and his memorial. It reflects the architectural and administrative genius of the Maratha empire
Rajgad Fort, Shivaji’s first capital before Raigad, is spread across multiple plateaus. It served as a launchpad for numerous battles and boasts intricate designs that balanced beauty with strategic defense
Pratapgad Fort, deep in the forests of Satara, is the site of the famous duel between Shivaji and Afzal Khan. The fort’s rugged terrain and hidden passages are testaments to guerrilla warfare techniques perfected by the Marathas.
Suvarnadurg Fort, located on a rocky island off the Konkan coast, was a naval base that allowed the Marathas to fend off Portuguese and British fleets. It’s surrounded by turquoise waters, making it a surreal blend of military strength and natural beauty.
Panhala Fort, in Kolhapur, is the largest Deccan fort and was used extensively by Shivaji and later Sambhaji. It includes the "Andhar Bavadi" (Hidden Well) and winding escape routes that helped during sieges.
Vijaydurg Fort stands tall on the Sindhudurg coast and was reputed to have underwater defenses. Once a formidable naval base, its triple-layered walls and strategic positioning made it almost impenetrable.
Sindhudurg Fort, built by Shivaji himself, is unique for housing a temple dedicated to him—an unusual honor for a ruler. Located on an island, the fort showcases ingenious construction techniques that blend seamlessly into the ocean’s rhythm.
Gingee Fort, the lone outlier in Tamil Nadu, is massive and awe-inspiring. Often dubbed the "Troy of the East," it comprises three separate hilltop citadels and served as a critical outpost for the Marathas in South India.
